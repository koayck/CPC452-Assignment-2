\section{Abstract}

Toxland VR: Hop \& Help is an educational Virtual Reality application that teaches young children to recognise and safely handle toxic or hazardous household materials. Inspired by the classic Snakes and Ladders board game, players take on the role of Junior Safety Rangers and progress across a giant interactive board representing the rooms of a home, making safety decisions about items such as cleaning chemicals, medicines, batteries, detergents, pesticides, and aerosol sprays. Correct decisions advance players via Good Practice ladders, while unsafe choices trigger Toxic Trap penalties accompanied by educational explanations. This project converts and adapts an existing Unity3D VR build developed with the Meta SDK for the Meta Quest headset into an XR-compatible build that runs on the PICO 4 headset using the PICO XR SDK. The conversion covers Unity XR settings, controller input mapping, camera rig and locomotion, UI interaction, and Android build configuration. This report documents the VR concept, background study, conversion methodology, system development, and testing evidence confirming that the application runs successfully on the target platform.

This section presents the conceptual design of Toxland VR: Hop \& Help, including its theme, intended audience, world design, storyline, storyboard, and team structure.

\subsection{Concept Theme}

The concept theme of Toxland VR is household hazard safety education, delivered through a board-game-inspired progression system. The game is a hybrid of an educational and entertainment-focused experience: it uses the familiar mechanics of Snakes and Ladders --- ``Good Practice'' ladders that reward safe behaviour and ``Toxic Trap'' penalties that punish unsafe behaviour --- to reinforce real-world household safety habits around cleaning chemicals, medicines, batteries, detergents, pesticides, and aerosol sprays.

The VR medium is chosen because immersive, first-person decision-making is expected to reinforce safety habits more effectively than passive instruction such as posters or textbook lessons, while the game-like reward/penalty structure keeps engagement age-appropriate.

\subsection{World Design}

Players enter the colourful world of Toxland, where everyday household items come to life. The board is divided into rooms representing different parts of a home:

\begin{itemize}
  \item Kitchen
  \item Bathroom
  \item Laundry area
  \item Garden
  \item Storage room
\end{itemize}

Each room hosts Good Practice Spaces, Bad Practice Spaces, and Learning Card spaces relevant to the hazards typically found in that part of the house \todo{insert environment concept art / world map screenshot once the 3D environment is built}.

\subsection{Storyline}

Players take on the role of Junior Safety Rangers tasked with travelling across the Toxland board to reach the Safety Champion Zone. Along the way they:

\begin{enumerate}
  \item Roll a virtual dice or teleport to the next board space.
  \item Encounter a household safety scenario at that space.
  \item Choose a safe or unsafe action via VR controller selection.
  \item Advance via a Good Practice ladder (safe choice) or fall back via a Toxic Trap (unsafe choice), with an educational explanation shown either way.
  \item Occasionally land on a Learning Card space, triggering a safety tip, fun fact, mini quiz, or hazard identification challenge.
\end{enumerate}

The game ends when a player reaches the Safety Champion Zone, at which point the final score is tallied and displayed on the end screen.

\subsection{Game Flow}

\begin{enumerate}
  \item \textbf{Welcome Screen} --- player is introduced to Toxland and the Junior Safety Ranger role.
  \item \textbf{Board Loop} --- player rolls the dice or teleports to the next space, the space's event (Good Practice, Bad Practice, or Learning Card) is resolved, and the score is updated.
  \item \textbf{Safety Champion Zone} --- reached when the player completes the board, ending the loop.
  \item \textbf{End Screen} --- final score and a summary of safety lessons learnt are displayed.
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Interactive Zone \& Triggers}

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Dice/teleport trigger} --- initiates player movement to the next board space.
  \item \textbf{Good Practice Space trigger} --- detects landing on a ladder space and grants bonus movement.
  \item \textbf{Bad Practice Space trigger} --- detects landing on a toxic trap space, applies a movement penalty, and displays an educational warning.
  \item \textbf{Learning Card trigger} --- spawns a safety tip, fun fact, mini quiz, or hazard identification challenge UI panel.
  \item \textbf{Score trigger} --- updates the score system on correct safety decisions, quiz answers, hazard identification, and game completion.
\end{itemize}
